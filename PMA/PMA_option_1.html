President's Management Agenda

Establishes and defines the Administration's key management reform priorities and initiatives



From Vision to Action: Committments

The Administration will make aggressive down payments on our vision, paving the way for sustained improvement over time. We will carry out this important work through multiple channels that, together, will yield tangible improvements for our country, people, and economy.

Cross-Agency Priority Goals

Cross-Agency Priority Goals have been established to drive implementation of the President’s Management Agenda and tackle these government-wide challenges. In March, the Administration will announce in greater detail specific efforts to advance the three drivers of reform, including senior accountable officials, concrete goals, and trackable metrics to ensure public accountability. This agenda will address critical challenges where government as a whole still operates in the past.

The President's Mangement Agenda: A Multi-Generational Vision for Reform

The President’s Management Agenda establishes and defines the Administration’s key management reform priorities and initiatives. This vision for reform must be multi-generational, equipping the Federal government to continue adapting to changing needs over time. We cannot pursue short-term fixes only to see government quickly become outdated yet again. Deep-seated transformation takes time and will not happen in one or two years. The long-term vision for effective government encompasses:


Improve Mission Delivery

The American people count on the Federal government every day, from national security to infrastructure to food and water safety. Public servants must be accountable for mission results and have the tools to deliver.

Provide Customers With a Modern Secure Experience

Federal customers range from small businesses seeking loans to families receiving disaster support to veterans owed proper benefits and medical care. They deserve a customer experience comparable to leading private organizations.

Direct taxpayer dollars to the most effective and efficient purposes.

Stewardship of taxpayer funds is a crucial responsibility, from preventing fraud to maximizing impact. Taxpayer dollars must go to effective programs that don’t waste money. The FY 2019 Budget projects that $170 billion in savings can be achieved over the next decade through reductions in improper payments alone.

Key Drivers of Reform

Many of the Federal government's challenges and shortcomings arise from practices designed in the past and trends that we can no longer sustain. Major root cause challenges facing the Federal government include structural issues, decision-making and processes, leadership and culture.

While the challenges are complex, a few key drivers will determine our succes at reform.

Modern information technology will serve as the backbone of how government serves the public and how we keep critical data and systems secure.

Data, accountability, and transparency provide the tools to deliver visibly better results to the public.

A 21st century workforce will enable senior leaders and front-line managers to align staff skills with mission needs. This will require reskilling and redeploying existing workers to keep pace with an environment of change.
